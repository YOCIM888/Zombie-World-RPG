# In-Depth Story Analysis: Complete Worldview and Character Guide of *Zombie World*

> Foreword: This analysis is based on all texts, dialogues, quest chains, and endings of the full version of *Zombie World*. It strives to rigorously restore each character's personality, motivations, causes, and fate. Some unresolved foreshadows will be left for the sequel to reveal.

---

## 1. Worldview

### 1.1 Three Years After the Apocalypse

It's the third year since the zombie virus outbreak. World order has completely collapsed, and survivors are struggling to survive in the ruins. You—the protagonist—are a wandering survivor with fragmented memories, huddled in a tattered tent, holding a piece of dry compressed biscuit and half a bottle of water. Most of the past has been washed away by chaos and fear.

This is not a simple story of humans fighting zombies. **The real battlefield has always been among people.**

### 1.2 The Tri-Power Structure

In the apocalypse, survivors have split into two opposing forces of order:

- **Doom Castle**: A feudal kingdom established by Karen, a former real estate tycoon. High walls, a noble hierarchy, cigarettes as currency, and a dictator king. The castle represents order, but order is bought with iron fists and hierarchy.
- **Dawn Outpost**: A civilian refuge established by a mysterious leader. Mutual support, collective labor, badges instead of identity cards. The outpost represents hope, but hope requires everyone to share the burden.
- **Neutral/Wandering Survivors**: Free people who don't belong to any force, such as the wanderers, black market merchants, mechanics, Old Wolf, etc., scattered across the wasteland. They represent another way to survive in the apocalypse—neither dependent nor obedient.

### 1.3 Dual Currency System

- **Cigarettes**: The hard currency of the apocalypse, a universal medium of exchange. The trading post at Dawn Outpost, black market merchants, and even Lilith's recycling service all settle in cigarettes.
- **Royal Coins**: The exclusive currency of the castle, used for the queen's grocery store, island bank, island investments, etc. Obtained through Duke Yumo's quest chain and material exchanges.

### 1.4 The Narrative Reflected in Core Survival Mechanics

Every survival value tells the cruelty of the apocalypse:

- **Satiety and Hydration**: Automatically decay every turn, and you lose HP when they reach zero—hunger is the first enemy of the apocalypse.
- **Infection Level**: You lose HP continuously if it exceeds 50%, and you die at 100%—the threat of zombification is always hanging over your head.
- **Sanity**: Exploring terrifying scenes reduces sanity, and attack damage is halved with a 50% chance when sanity reaches zero—mental breakdown is more terrifying than physical death.
- **Fatigue**: Overexertion leads to a state of fatigue—there are no perpetual motion machines in the apocalypse; rest is a necessity.

## 2. In-Depth Analysis of Core Characters

### 2.1 King Karen: A Tyrant Consumed by Power

**Identity**: Ruler of Doom Castle, former real estate tycoon.
**Key Personality Traits**: Greedy, vain, extremely controlling, with his own imperial logic.

**In-Depth Interpretation**:
The king is the most complex villain in the game. He's not just a "evil tyrant"—he's an ordinary person whose human weaknesses have been extremely amplified by the apocalypse.

"Before the apocalypse, I was a real estate tycoon; after the apocalypse, I'm the king of the castle!"—this line sums up his essence. Real estate tycoons are good at enclosing land, pricing, and monopolizing; the king's essence is to replicate this business logic into apocalyptic rule.

He establishes a noble hierarchy (Noble → Viscount → Count → Marquis → Duke → Crown Prince), and every step of promotion requires you to hand over materials—glucose solution (to treat the queen), high-proof liquor (to hold banquets), cigarettes and beer (for personal enjoyment), universal injections (to pursue immortality), assassinating the outpost leader (to eliminate dissidents). **The progression of these quests perfectly demonstrates how a tyrant slides from reasonable needs to madness step by step**:

1. Treating the queen—seems affectionate and righteous
2. Holding banquets—the beginning of hedonism
3. Cigarettes and alcohol—addiction and neglect of governance
4. Universal injections—the obsession with immortality, the ultimate fear of power is being replaced
5. Assassinating the outpost leader—**the ultimate logic of power: eliminate all potential threats**

In the palace garden, he even erected a monument to commemorate fallen soldiers—this is the only trace of human warmth he shows.

Is his love for the queen real? The queen's answer gives the answer: "He only loves me. In an apocalypse, a king only guards his queen."—this is a twisted romance. He gave her a crown, a castle, and all his heart, but at the same time built high walls, separating her from her own brother for three years.

**Ending Hint**: The king will eventually grow old, and will you—the crown prince—become the next him? This is the game's sharpest question about "power inheritance."

### 2.2 The Queen: A Nameless Flower Between Two Walls

**Identity**: Queen of the castle, biological sister of the outpost leader, former supermodel.
**Key Personality Traits**: Softness beneath arrogance, contradiction, forbearance, sober pain.

**In-Depth Interpretation**:
The queen's story is one of the most touching emotional arcs in the game.

Her coldness to strangers—"Hmph, another beggar. Stay away from me, your clothes are filthy."—is self-protection. As a former supermodel, she built a wall with sharpness in the apocalypse. But as quests progress, you'll find that all her "noble arrogance" is just a protective color.

Her cherishing of the **sunflower pendant** reveals everything: when the king was still a real estate tycoon, he made this pendant himself and gave it to her. "Sunflowers always face the sun, just like I always look at you."—back then, he was still human.

Her quest chain (dispelling sorrow → king's recent situation → sword practice → wedding anniversary) peels back layer by layer her relationship with the king:
- Dispelling sorrow: Wants liquor. She needs to numb herself to face those unpleasant memories.
- King's recent situation: Wants wolfberries. She still cares about the king's health. **A woman caring about someone she may no longer love** is a shackle of responsibility.
- Sword practice: Wants a rapier. The king's only hobby is practicing swordsmanship. "Only with a sword in hand can you protect those you want to protect"—but she retorts: "But he protected everyone, except himself."
- Wedding anniversary: Wants an M700. Giving her husband his favorite gun. But the hidden thread is—she needs a sunflower pendant (a token of love) to reawaken the king's humanity.

When you tell her brother the truth about "that girl" from the voice recorder, and then connect the two through the leader's letter and reply, the queen's tragedy of standing between two high walls reaches its peak: "Brother, I don't hate you. But some wounds, even time can't heal."

**She is a woman trapped in imperial love. She chose to stay in the castle not because of forgiveness, but because she no longer has the strength to choose again.**

### 2.3 The Outpost Leader: A Martyr Moving Forward with Guilt

**Identity**: Founder and leader of Dawn Outpost, biological brother of the queen.
**Key Personality Traits**: Resolute, silent, self-reproachful, idealistic, still choosing to wait even after being betrayed.

**In-Depth Interpretation**:
The leader is the "light of justice" in the game, but he's not a saint without weaknesses. His core motivation is—**guilt**.

The voice recorder reveals the truth: three years ago, when the king was still an ordinary businessman, he took a fancy to the leader's sister. The leader opposed this relationship because he "didn't trust that person." Later, his intuition was proven correct—the king showed his true colors, tried to control outpost resources, and held a grudge after being rejected by the leader.

But the price paid for this is: the sister left heartbroken and became the queen of the castle. The leader wrote letters for three years, never sending them. **He protected the outpost, but lost his sister.**

His quest chain (voice recorder → reply → alcohol poisoning → treatment) is self-punishing:
- Wants high-proof liquor, needs to get drunk to face the content of the reply
- In the reply, the sister says "some wounds, even time can't heal"—he almost breaks down
- Alcohol poisoning, needs a universal injection to save him

He is the only NPC in the entire game who "drinks himself to the point of needing first aid."

Then he hands you the token of love (the sunflower pendant). This pendant was made by him for his sister, given by her to the king, and then lost by the king at the outpost—**a token, three people, all missed.**

After the leader's favorability reaches full, he awards you the "Dawn Captain Badge," which means he has already regarded you as a new hope. If he dies (assassinated), his last words are "You... why did you do this..."—this is the ultimate fatal blow of being betrayed by someone he trusted.

### 2.4 Miss V: Warm Light Beneath the Ice Blade

**Identity**: Former special forces soldier, core member of Dawn Outpost, lone assassin.
**Key Personality Traits**: Cold, taciturn, powerful, deep sense of responsibility, warmth wrapped in coldness.

**In-Depth Interpretation**:
Miss V is the ceiling of combat power at the outpost. Her dialogue stages perfectly demonstrate the entire process from "stranger → acquaintance → friend → close friend → soul mate":
- Stranger stage: "Don't get too close." "Strangers are not welcome here."
- Acquaintance stage: "You're still alive, not bad." "Don't wander around here, be careful of zombies."
- Friend stage: "You've improved a lot." (a rare compliment)
- Close friend stage: "I used to be in special forces... forget it, let's not talk about that."
- Soul mate: "Finally... found you." "I've left everything to you."

Her quest chain (first meeting quest → hemostasis training → serum quest → difficult trial → first aid supplies → ultimate trial) is a complete "boot camp." From a small dagger to a P226 to an Uzi to an M4A1, every level of bounty is pushing you to become stronger. **Miss V is not just rewarding you; she's personally training you to be someone who can take over her place to protect the outpost.**

Her rejection of castle forces is extremely resolute. If you have a castle identity, she will directly refuse to talk to you—this fits her military personality: clear stance, no compromise.

She once said: "If I die, take care of yourself for me."—this is her deepest fear: afraid that after she falls, no one will protect the outpost.

### 2.5 Su Xiaohan: Candy of the Apocalypse

**Identity**: Former kindergarten teacher, the warmest soul at the outpost.
**Key Personality Traits**: Naive, kind, timid but brave, longing for all beautiful things.

**In-Depth Interpretation**:
Su Xiaohan is the most "healing" character in the entire game. Her milk, pudding, and homemade amulets are all extremely scarce tenderness in the apocalypse.

But her naivety is not ignorance. After becoming close friends, she will say: "Tell you a secret... actually, I'm very afraid of zombies..." "You know, I used to be a kindergarten teacher, those kids were so cute..." "If only we could go back to the past..."

**She remembers every child.**—this is why she desperately collects milk and food, why she turns all rewards into materials that can be distributed to others at the outpost. She is rebuilding a kindergarten, a kindergarten that can never go back.

Her rejection of castle forces is also nuanced. It's not Miss V's cold rejection, but: "You... you're from the castle? I thought you were a good person... Mom said people from the castle are all bad..."—**like a child told by adults "bad guys look like this"**. Then her eyes redden, and she turns to leave. Making you not angry, but heartbroken.

Her highest-level quest (big shopping spree) requires submitting one of every drink and every food—she's hoarding the hope of survival for everyone at the outpost.

### 2.6 Lilith: An Engineer in the Ruins

**Identity**: A quirky girl, weapon recycling expert, mechanic, infection therapist.
**Key Personality Traits**: Mischievous, playful, smart, attachment to objects, hidden fear.

**In-Depth Interpretation**:
Lilith may be the most practical NPC in the game, but she's more than just a collection of functions.

Her mischief is a form of post-traumatic stress. She's always "picking things up"—baseball bats, fire axes, wrenches, old parts—**this is a transfer of control. In a world where the apocalypse can't be controlled, she builds a sense of security by "collecting objects."**

She sneaks out of the outpost at night to pick things up, she knows a "secret base"—these are not playfulness, but a teenage girl building a sense of control in her own way in the apocalypse.

Her ability setting is also extremely powerful: weapon recycling (melee/ranged), gun repair (50% integrity), crossbow repair, infection clearing (50% infection level), medical supply trading... **The youngest member of the outpost is actually the technical core of the outpost.**

After becoming close friends, she will say: "Actually... I'm a little afraid of the dark..." "You know, I used to want to be an engineer..." "If only there were no zombies in the world, I really want to go to school..."

In the final soul mate stage, she summons the courage to confess: "I... I like you! Will you be with me?"—how much courage does it take for a girl who hides all her fear with mischief to finally say this to you?

Her rejection of castle forces is also childish: "People from the castle are all bad guys! They steal our supplies! I don't want to play with you! Go away, go away!"

### 2.7 Mu Miaomiao: A Monument in the Soil

**Identity**: Doctor of botany, the only agricultural expert at the outpost, guardian of apocalyptic seeds.
**Key Personality Traits**: Calm, focused, paranoid tenderness, regarding planting as a belief.

**In-Depth Interpretation**:
Mu Miaomiao's story is the closest to a "pastoral poem" in the entire game, but it's full of pathos at its core.

Her diary (obtainable through exploration) reveals her trauma: when the apocalypse came, she watched her family starve to death. She couldn't grow anything, couldn't even keep a single seed alive. Later she learned—**every seed, every watering, every seedling is a form of atonement for the deceased family members.**

Her quest chain is extremely beautiful:
1. **Let's Go Picnic Together**: Wants carrots and potatoes. "This is the best soup I've had since the apocalypse."—simple enough to make people cry.
2. **Thoughts Across Time**: Wants high-proof liquor and rye. She takes you to the wreath tombstone to pay respects to her parents. Liquor is sprinkled in front of the tombstone, rye is scattered on the soil. "Look, Mom and Dad, I've already grown it."
3. **Expand the Experimental Field**: Collect 15 types of crops. She expands the experimental field three times and gives you the Prairie Bow—made from the toughest plants in the experimental field. "No one will lose their family to hunger again."

After her favorability is full, she will take you to a secret flower field—in the valley behind the outpost, sunflowers, lavender, and daisies are stubbornly growing in the gaps of the ruins. She hands you the Agricultural Master Badge left by her mentor.

**In the ending "I'm Not the God of Agriculture"**, she says it best: "I'm not some god of agriculture... I'm just an ordinary person who doesn't want anyone to go hungry again."

She's not trying to conquer nature; she's just trying to heal those memories torn apart by the apocalypse. Every crop is a monument.

### 2.8 Old Wolf and the Injured Mermaid: A Legend of the Apocalypse

**Identity**: Old Wolf—a lonely elderly person in the residential area, holder of a shotgun, guardian of ocean legends. Mermaid—a deep-sea creature trapped in the property warehouse, injured and dying.

**Key Personality Traits**:
- Old Wolf: Suspicious, stubborn, hidden, with a sense of ancient mission.
- Mermaid: Fragile, pure, grateful, tenderness from another dimension.

**In-Depth Interpretation**:
Old Wolf is one of the most mysterious characters in the game. He lives alone in the residential area and scolds everyone he meets, "In this world, people are scarier than zombies"—until you help him complete his quest, you'll find that what he's really guarding is not himself, but the injured mermaid in the property warehouse.

His character design reminds people of *The Old Man and the Sea*—an old man accompanied by deep-sea secrets.

The mermaid is the only "non-human intelligent creature" in the game. The fish tail on her lower body has a deep wound, and most of her scales have fallen off. You feed her lightning scales and thunder scales obtained from the electric fish tank, and the scales turn into thunder energy that seeps into the wound, slowly healing it.

**Note: How to obtain scales**—put rare fish in the electric fish tank in the mall, and the current will refine the scales. This is a metaphor for "resurrecting ancient creatures with apocalyptic technology." The energy of the apocalypse (electricity, radiation) not only created zombies, but may also awaken sleeping ancient creatures.

After the mermaid's favorability is full, she gives you the **Sea Song Trident**—"A sacred artifact of our clan. It once belonged to my mother, now... it belongs to you."

**Ending "Mermaid Legend"**: Deep under the sea sleeps a "giant sea corpse," a body like a rotting whale covered in black scales, with dozens of tentacles each ending in a faint glowing eye. The energy of the apocalypse awakened it. Protected by the mermaid, you and Old Wolf go out to sea together and use long-range weapons to send it back to the abyss. The mermaid returns to the deep sea, leaving you two scales—"No matter where you go, the sea will protect you."

The secrets under the sea, the awakening of ancient creatures, the friendship between humans and non-human races in the apocalypse—this is a dark thread with strong fantasy elements.

### 2.9 Liu Ruyan: A Girl Picked Up from Hell

**Identity**: Your junior corresponding to your senior identity, a survivor rescued after being infected.
**Key Personality Traits**: Resilient, intelligent, planned, romantic adventurous spirit.

**In-Depth Interpretation**:
Liu Ruyan is a semi-infected woman you found in the **abandoned university**. Her skin has started to fester, but her eyes still retain the light of humanity. She has a **30-day life-and-death countdown**—if you don't inject her with anti-infection serum within 30 days, she will completely turn into a zombie.

The entire Liu Ruyan quest chain is a complete love narrative arc:
1. **First Meeting**: Inject serum, save her. She weakly says, "My name is Liu Ruyan... if you don't mind... I want to follow you."
2. **Old Classroom**: She recognizes you as the senior two grades above her. Takes you to the infirmary to find the universal injection.
3. **Stadium**: Fight the rugby zombie (infected school rugby team captain). She watches from the side—"You did it!"
4. **Administration Building**: Pry the lock open with a crowbar to get the off-road vehicle keys left by the principal.
5. **Gas Station**: Find mechanic Wang Tiezhu to exchange for gasoline, refuel the off-road vehicle.

**Ending "Elopement"**: The two of you drive the off-road vehicle out of the city. The trunk is stuffed with a few bottles of water, some compressed biscuits, and a crowbar. Liu Ruyan leans her head against the car window: "Before the apocalypse, my biggest wish was a graduation trip." You step on the gas: "Then now is our graduation trip." She smiles with her eyes curved.

The power of this line lies in: it reconstructs the apocalyptic escape into the purest romance of youth. **When the whole world is collapsing, there's still someone willing to escape with you—this is the greatest luxury in the apocalypse.**

### 2.10 Duke Yumo: A Banished Pawn

**Identity**: Island duke enfeoffed by the king, highest person in charge of the island area.
**Key Personality Traits**: Loyal to the point of foolishness, anxious, lonely, pain of being marginalized.

**In-Depth Interpretation**:
Duke Yumo is a very tragic character in the king's system. Before the apocalypse, he was just an ordinary civil servant—"Who would have thought that one day I'd become a duke?"

His loyalty to the king is almost religious. When you first meet him, he says "His Majesty the King is the wisest leader I've ever met," "I'm willing to go through fire and water for His Majesty the King, I'll do anything." But as favorability increases, his words gradually reveal flaws:

"You know, being a duke isn't as glamorous as outsiders think... every step is like walking on thin ice."
"His Majesty the King has the grace of knowing me... but sometimes, I also doubt the meaning of all this."
"This island... actually hides a lot of secrets."

The logic of his quest chain is worth pondering: to complete the king's performance evaluation (merit), he needs zombie gel for research, research attracts diving sea monsters, after clearing the sea monsters he's in a hurry to build a fortress to show political achievements... **This is an apocalyptic official driven by performance evaluation**, the king manages him with KPIs, and he lives in a cycle of constantly "making achievements → anxious → making more achievements."

In the last quest (celebration banquet), he finally lets his guard down and drinks with you—"The king promised me that when everything is over, he'll transfer me back from here." He smiles bitterly, "But I know that day may never come."

**What is Yumo's essence? A person banished to a lonely island, forever waiting to be recalled.** Enfeoffment is not honor, but disguised exile.

### 2.11 Gu Yue and Lin Han: Brainwashed Subjects

**Character Interpretation**:

**Gu Yue** (island resident) and **Lin Han** (island resident) are two typical projections of the king's ideology among the common people:

- **Gu Yue (Fanatical Fan Type)**: "His Majesty the King single-handedly defeated a group of zombies!" "His Majesty the King's kindness is unmatched!" "Without His Majesty the King, how would there be any order in this apocalyptic world?"—all her words come from hearsay, but she chooses to believe them deeply.
- **Lin Han (Rational Supporter Type)**: "The king's strategy is very far-sighted, using cigarettes as currency to unify the trade system." "The identity card system implemented by the king not only ensures the safety of the castle but also gives survivors a path to promotion."—he will find a reasonable explanation for every decision of the king.

The two represent two typical ways for power to maintain rule: **deification narrative (Gu Yue) and system justification (Lin Han)**.

### 2.12 Dr. Chen: Sin and Redemption

**Identity**: Virologist at the biochemical research institute, the only hope for rocket repair.
**Key Personality Traits**: Focused, paranoid, dedicated, half scientist half redeemer.

**In-Depth Interpretation**:
Dr. Chen is the most important "neutral intellectual NPC" in the game. His appearance is accompanied by a research topic that has been studied for three years—the antidote to the zombie virus.

When you first meet him, he trades 30 serums for an M700 sniper rifle. But this deal is far more than just surface level: he needs serum to continue his research, and at the same time needs weapons to protect himself—which means he knows someone will come looking for him.

Every step of the rocket quest chain is tied to his personal beliefs:
1. Small nuclear generator—repair the energy mining well
2. 15 pure energy sources—replenish the rocket's propulsion system
3. Medical/food/drink reserves—survival supplies prepared for those taking the rocket

His final choice reveals the whole truth: if you don't board the rocket, he will go up himself. "I'm leaving. I left the manufacturing equipment for the improved antibody injection... stay alive."—**He didn't ask anyone's consent, maybe he never thought to ask.**

Old Ma's quest chain reveals another side: Old Ma hates him. The reason for hatred is "Why hasn't this apocalypse ended after three years? It's because of scientists like him! They're always researching viruses there, researching and researching, and there are more and more zombies!"—this sentence may be a paranoid conspiracy theory, but it also hints at a chilling possibility: **Dr. Chen's research may not be "solving" the virus, but "using" the virus.**

### 2.13 Old Ma: The Reckoner of the Old World

**Identity**: Factory warehouse guard, former accountant before the apocalypse, "enforcer of justice" in the apocalypse.
**Key Personality Traits**: Insidious, calculating, self-proclaimed righteous, using all available resources.

**In-Depth Interpretation**:
Old Ma is the deepest dark thread in the entire game. On the surface, he's a warehouse guard who trades building materials for weapons, but his real identity is—**an executioner who takes settling old scores as his mission in the apocalypse.**

Triggering his quest chain requires a key prerequisite: in the hidden compartment of the warehouse supermarket, find and use that tube of "apocalyptic contraband" emitting a faint green light.

After using it, your sanity collapses (-50), you see a blood moon, an upside-down city, countless silent screaming faces. When you get up, you feel your body has never been stronger—but also like something has been gouged out of you.

**Then Old Ma finds you.** He gives you a seven-step cleaning list:

1. **Shadow**: The masked man in the warehouse supermarket, one of the masterminds of the contraband transportation case back then.
2. **Ma San**: A cannibal, responsible for "handling" disobedient people.
3. **King**: The umbrella who used power to cover up the dark forces.
4. **Old Zhao**: A retired veteran who killed civilians under the guise of "safety inspection."
5. **Dr. Chen**: A scientist from the biochemical research institute, Old Ma believes he is the source of the virus spread.

Then, the seventh step—**he goes to the underground subway tunnel to wait for you and gives you 30 cigarettes.**

**Ending "Being Used as a Gun"**: Old Ma shoots you in the chest in the tunnel. "Shadow, Ma San, the king, Old Zhao, Dr. Chen... you've carried too many lives. Every one of their deaths is recorded in my ledger. I'm just... enforcing justice on behalf of heaven."

**This is the most exquisite narrative trap in the entire game.** You thought you were the protagonist, a hero讨伐 evil; Old Ma made you think he was a mentor of justice. But in the end you'll find—you're just a bullet in his gun. He counted you into the "cleaning list" from the very beginning.

And how many lives does Old Ma's own ledger carry? Who will settle his score?

### 2.14 Ma San: The Purest Evil

**Identity**: A tattooed strong man at the national highway expressway service area—a cannibal.
**Key Personality Traits**: Rough, bloodthirsty, regards cannibalism as an art, crazy to the point of hair-raising.

**In-Depth Interpretation**:
Ma San is the embodiment of "pure evil" in the game. He doesn't have the logic of power like the king, nor the excuse of revenge like Old Ma. He eats people just because he wants to.

His quest chain is a process that step by step leads you to hell:
1. Use power beer—"testing new flavors"
2. Use crops—beer stewed pork ribs
3. Use cigarettes—"I have a huge armory! I'll give you an RPG rocket launcher!" (a lie)
4. Go to the armory with him—"I want to treat you as a fellow, good brother!"

**If you accept the fourth step's invitation, the ending "Becoming a Meal" is triggered.** You will be anesthetized, dismembered, and cooked into another "new dish" of his.

The terrifying thing about this ending is: every word Ma San said before was true, and also false. He said he wanted to rule the area with you, he said he had an armory, he said you were his brother—**he does have the ambition to "rule the area" (rule your body), has an "armory" (his kitchen knife), you are his "brother" (brother on the chopping block).**

The scariest lie is that every word is true.

### 2.15 Shadow: The Shadow of the Dark Side

**Identity**: Masked gunman at the warehouse supermarket, core of the apocalyptic contraband transportation chain.
**Key Personality Traits**: Silent, brutal, an existence in the shadows.

**Interpretation**: Shadow has very few lines (only one "looking for death"), but his position in the entire old world dark force network is crucial—he is the supplier. He, Ma San, and the king together form the criminal ecosystem before the apocalypse: Shadow supplies → Ma San "handles" → the king covers up. Old Ma, as the accountant back then, has all the records and settles scores one by one.

### 2.16 Old Zhao: A Man Destroyed by War

**Identity**: Retired veteran at the military checkpoint, PTSD patient.
**Key Personality Traits**: Paranoid, manic, potentially dangerous but not purely evil.

**Interpretation**: Old Zhao is the NPC that makes players most conflicted in the game. Sometimes he gives you ammunition, sometimes he suddenly goes manic and fires a stray shot (50% chance of accidental injury). He doesn't mean to hurt you—**his mind has been destroyed by war.**

Old Ma accuses him of "killing civilians under the guise of safety inspection"—but this may be Old Ma's one-sided story. Old Zhao represents: **How the destruction of humanity by war turns from "protector" to "perpetrator".**

---

### 2.17 Luluwei: A Bridge Between Zombies and Humans

**Identity**: A female zombie wearing a tattered nurse's uniform, with the name "Luluwei" faintly visible on her chest badge.
**Key Personality Traits**: Remaining humanity, docile, obsession with canned food.

**Interpretation**: Luluwei is the only "zombie who retains sanity" in the entire game. You accumulate favorability by feeding her canned food (she likes canned food, doesn't like ordinary food), and after reaching 150, you can bring her back to the base as a companion.

Her significance is far more than just a "companion system":
- She proves that the zombie virus is not a 100% "death sentence"—some people can retain part of their humanity
- In the ending "Becoming a New Force", she becomes a "bridge connecting humans and mutants" and combines with Dr. Chen's research results
- Her process from zombie to regaining sanity hints at the possibility of reversing zombification

The name on her chest badge—Luluwei—is one of the few zombies with a name in the game, and the identity setting as a nurse is very interesting: a former caregiver, now abandoned by the whole world.

### 2.18 Wang Tiezhu: The Wasteland Mechanic

**Identity**: Mechanic at the abandoned gas station in the suburbs, technician before the apocalypse.
**Personality**: Pragmatic, enthusiastic, cherishes everything that can still be repaired.

**Interpretation**: Wang Tiezhu is the spokesperson for "technical democratization." A large wrench or iron pipe can be exchanged for ammunition, food, and water. 5 cigarettes for 1 gasoline. He is a pragmatist completely游离于 the power structure—doesn't care who is ruling, only cares about "what was fixed today."

### 2.19 Corpse King · Silence and the Giant Sea Corpse: Natural Disasters of the Apocalypse

**Interpretation**:

- **Corpse King · Silence** (420 HP, 115 damage): The true ruler of the zombie nest, three times the size of an ordinary zombie, skin like black armor, body covered in weapon remnants—every embedded object is a relic of a challenger it defeated.
- **Giant Sea Corpse** (600 HP, 80 damage): An ancient creature that slept in the deep sea for hundreds of years, awakened by the energy of the apocalypse. Can only be attacked remotely.

These two represent enemies at the "natural disaster" level in the game: they are not humans, not conspiracies, but **products of this twisted world**. The zombie king is an ultimate killing machine artificially created, and the giant sea corpse is an ancient existence awakened by energy pollution.

## 3. Analysis of Main Storylines

### 3.1 Castle vs Outpost: Two Sides of the Mirror of Power and Order

This is the core political philosophy proposition of the game: **In the apocalypse, what kind of order can let humans survive?**

- **Castle Mode**: Strongman politics + feudal hierarchy + capitalist currency system. The advantages are efficiency, stability, and a clear promotion channel. The cost is exploiting the bottom, a huge gap between nobles and common people, and the absolute corruption of power.
- **Outpost Mode**: Grassroots democracy + mutual aid labor + socialist community. The advantages are equality, warmth, and everyone being respected. The cost is lower efficiency, the leader being too introverted, and being more vulnerable in the face of power.

But the game doesn't simply praise or criticize either side. The castle has the queen's true feelings, the peace of the garden, and a moment of respite in the guest room and dance; the outpost has assassination (the king's quest) and begging (a survival method with low dignity but high returns).

More importantly—**The leaders of both modes made the same mistake: loving in the way they thought was right, but hurting the people closest to them.** The king tied the queen with a crown, and the leader broke up his sister and her lover with opposition. Their different orders point to the same tragedy.

---

### 3.2 Leader-Queen-King: An Apocalyptic Triangular Tragedy

This is the most exquisite emotional narrative structure in the game:

```
King ←—Marriage—→ Queen ←—Bloodline—→ Leader
                                ↓
                         Oppose them being together
                                ↓
                         Sister leaves, becomes queen
                                ↓
                    Leader writes letters for three years, never sends them
```

Three characters, two relationships, three years of silence.

The leader's voice recorder reveals the truth: the intelligence agent sent by the king was killed. That girl did nothing wrong. The leader's regret, the queen's forgiveness, the king's possessiveness—three form an emotional dead knot that cannot be untied.

The sunflower pendant—the king's handiwork, worn by the queen for three years, left at the outpost for three years—is the perfect symbol of this triangular relationship: **A flower facing the sun, but unable to bring real sunlight.**

---

### 3.3 Old Ma's Revenge Chain: The Anti-Hero Judge

Old Ma's quest chain is the darkest and most exquisite narrative design in the game.

Surface logic: A former accountant who knows "insider information" hires you to clean up old world criminals. Every time you kill someone, he gives you cigarettes as a reward.

Actual logic: **He's using you to eliminate everyone who can testify against him.** You are the last piece of the puzzle for him to hide the past. When you complete all assassinations, you become the last piece of evidence.

He proclaims himself "enforcing justice on behalf of heaven," but his "way of heaven" is just another excuse for killing. He's smarter than the king, more forbearing than Ma San, more patient than Shadow—**He is the most dangerous type of person in the apocalypse: a villain with beliefs.**

His ending line is brilliant: "Don't blame me. In this apocalypse, someone has to settle this account."—he has no trace of remorse.

---

### 3.4 Liu Ruyan's Elopement Arc: Romanticism of the Apocalypse

This is the only pure love narrative in the game.

From saving a dying semi-infected person, to revisiting the old classroom of the alma mater to recall the past, to fighting the rugby zombie, prying the door to get the keys, finding gasoline to refuel, and finally driving into the distance—the rhythm of this arc is: **Two people rebuilding a complete life trajectory step by step in the ruins.**

Liu Ruyan's story answers a core question in apocalyptic literature: **When the world ends, does love still hold?**

The answer is in the roar of the off-road vehicle's engine, in her saying "Before the apocalypse, my biggest wish was a graduation trip" when the setting sun dyes the sky orange, and in your reply "Then now is our graduation trip."

---

### 3.5 Old Wolf and the Mermaid: Myth Reborn in the Ruins

This side arc is the most "fantasy" part of the game. It's completely different from the main zombie narrative—it explores: **The energy of the apocalypse not only destroyed human civilization, but may also awaken older existences than humans.**

The injured mermaid, the giant sea corpse in the deep sea, scales that grow with electricity—these elements remind people of Lovecraftian ocean horror, but the narrative is more偏向 redemption like *The Little Mermaid*. The mermaid's return is not power, but gratitude—"I will remember you—people who are willing to help a mermaid in this cursed world."

Old Wolf's motivation for guarding the warehouse has always been a mystery. How did he find the mermaid? Why does he know the secret of the scales? The game hasn't answered these questions yet.

---

### 3.6 Duke Yumo's Island Fief: The Marginalization of Power

Yumo's story is a micro-sample of "how the feudal system operates in the apocalypse":

1. King enfeoffs → for border stability
2. Yumo needs achievements → to not have his fief reclaimed
3. Collect zombie gel for research → for achievements
4. Research attracts sea monsters → to solve research problems
5. Build sea fortress → to show political achievements to the king
6. Drink celebration wine → finally let guard down

*His five quests are interlocking, and every completion is a compromise to "the fear of being forgotten"**: Collect gel → research → attract sea monsters → build fortress to show → drink to numb. The tighter he's crushed in the power machine, the more he depends on you—and the more you help him, the sadder he becomes.

At that final celebration banquet, he says the three loneliest lines in the entire game:
1. "His Majesty the King also came here for vacation before. Back then, he was just a successful businessman. We met at that time."
2. "He promised me that when everything is over, he'll transfer me back from here."
3. "But I know that day may never come."

**This is the truth of power: not a grand conquest, but an infinite postponement of "when everything is over."**

---

### 3.7 Mu Miaomiao's Seeds: A Monument to Memory

Mu Miaomiao's quest chain is the closest to a "requiem" in the entire game.

The narrative rhythm is like picking up a water-soaked diary from the ruins, turning page by page—from a simple picnic, to a heavy tomb-sweeping, to a grand expansion of the experimental field. Every quest is fighting the same enemy: **Forgetting.**

She scatters rye in front of the tombstone, strokes every crop in the experimental field—this is not farming, this is fighting death in the oldest way. The land buries the dead, the land grows food, the food feeds the living. This is the most fundamental life cycle in the apocalypse.

Her secret flower field—in the valley behind the outpost, sunflowers, lavender, and daisies stubbornly growing in the gaps of the ruins—is the most beautiful scene in the entire game. Not because the flowers are好看, but because the existence of this flower field itself is a declaration: **In the ugliest corner of the world, there are still people choosing to guard beauty.**

The Agricultural Master Badge left by her mentor, she hands to you. This is a trust so heavy it's almost hard to take—not "help me guard," but "now it's your turn to remember."

**Mu Miaomiao is not the god of agriculture, but she is the last person who still remembers the names of the seeds.**

---

### 3.8 The Rocket and the Doctor: A Bet on Civilization

The rocket quest chain is the only arc in the game that points to the "outside world."

Dr. Chen spent three years repairing the rocket. You need to defeat the giant puppet at the space base, obtain the small nuclear generator from the king (must join the castle), extract 15 pure energy sources, and reserve enough survival supplies. Every step is asking the same question: **How much are you willing to pay to leave this planet?**

When the rocket takes off, the game gives you three choices:
1. Go yourself—fly into space alone and become a lonely cosmic drifter.
2. Take important people with you—requires Miss V, Su Xiaohan, and Lilith to all reach soul mate level (150 favorability).
3. I won't go, let the doctor go—the doctor flies into space with the research results, and you stay to continue fighting.

**Option 3 is the doctor's original intention.** He never intended to ask anyone's consent from the beginning. He says "I'm leaving, I left the manufacturing equipment for the improved antibody injection... stay alive." This is not a scientist's farewell, this is a debtor's repayment.

Old Ma accuses him "it's because of scientists like him that the virus spread"—this sentence may be a conspiracy theory, but it may also contain the biggest foreshadowing in the game: **Where did the virus come from? Is Dr. Chen's research an antidote or the origin?**

The game doesn't give a clear answer. But the doctor's back as he boards the rocket and flies into space alone is like a fugitive taking all secrets away, and also like a martyr leaving the last hope to the world.

---

### 3.9 Luluwei: Proof of Zombies and Humanity

Luluwei's existence is the gentlest paradox in the entire game.

You found her in the large suburban hospital—wearing a tattered nurse's uniform, with "Luluwei" faintly visible on her chest badge. She's a zombie, but she likes canned food and doesn't like ordinary food. She won't attack you.

You feed her 50~75 cans, and her favorability climbs from 0 to 150. Then—she comes home with you. Gives you a random medicine every day, and automatically restores 20 HP after winning a battle.

But her significance is far more than just a "companion system":
- She proves that the zombie virus is not a 100% death sentence—some people retain part of their humanity due to some unknown factor.
- In ending five "Becoming a New Force", she becomes a "bridge connecting humans and mutants."
- Her identity setting as a nurse hints that the instinct of care may have overcome the instinct of zombification.

**In a world where all zombies are enemies, Luluwei is a crack—a crack through which the light of "maybe zombies can also be saved" shines.**

## 4. In-Depth Interpretation of Endings

The game has a total of 14 endings, which can be divided into six categories. Every ending is not a simple "good or bad" label, but an answer to different survival philosophies of the apocalypse.

### 4.1 Failure Endings

#### Ending 1: Death
The most straightforward ending. You died without doing anything meaningful. The significance of this ending is to tell players: **The apocalypse doesn't accept any romanticized imagination. Death is death, no tombstone, no memorial service, just the next corpse.**

---

### 4.2 Trap Endings

#### Ending 11: Becoming a Meal
Follow Ma San into the "armory," get anesthetized, dismembered, cooked. The horror of this ending is not in the blood, but in the **narrative trick**: Ma San was telling the truth the whole time—"rule the area together" (rule your body), "armory" (kitchen knife), "brother" (brother on the chopping block). You had enough information to judge the danger from the beginning, but greed and credulity made you choose to ignore it.

**This is the game's most accurate restoration of "bait-style deception."**

#### Ending 14: Being Used as a Gun
After completing Old Ma's seven assassination quests, get shot dead by him in the tunnel. Old Ma's line reveals the truth: "You've carried too many lives. Every one of their deaths is recorded in my ledger. I'm just... enforcing justice on behalf of heaven."

You eliminated five "bad guys," but became the sixth "piece of evidence." Old Ma counted you into the cleaning list from the very beginning. His "enforcing justice on behalf of heaven" is a perfect excuse for cyclic killing.

**The shock of this ending is: you thought you were the hunter, but you've actually always been the prey. Even worse—you're just a tool, used and discarded.**

---

### 4.3 Power Endings

#### Ending 3: The Addiction to Power
Triggered after obtaining the crown prince identity card and going home. You've already gotten used to the luxury of the castle—the king's appreciation, the ministers' flattery, the servants' awe. But there's a piercing question hidden in the ending text: "The king will eventually grow old. And you... are you ready to become the next him?"

Power is not a line, it's a circle. The king's ending is the crown prince's future. **Between becoming a hero and becoming a new tyrant, there's only a thought's difference.**

#### Ending 4: Shine Bright
Triggered after obtaining the Dawn Captain Badge and going home. You need to make Miss V, Su Xiaohan, Lilith, Mu Miaomiao, and the outpost leader's favorability all reach the highest—this means you've truly integrated into this community.

"In the apocalypse, you are that warmest light."—this is the only ending in the game that makes you a **true hero**. But it's not retirement after achieving fame and success, but "tomorrow, you still have to continue leading everyone to scavenge materials, reinforce defenses, and rescue survivors."

**The outpost's hero has no holidays. A hero is a responsibility, not a title.**

#### Ending 5: Becoming a New Force
The hardest ending to trigger in the entire game, requires meeting multiple conditions at the same time: Luluwei recruited, Liu Ruyan rescued, Ma San killed, Shadow killed, Corpse King defeated, all three of Dr. Chen's rocket quests completed.

This ending is a new triangular balance: Luluwei's transformed "sober zombie" troops + Liu Ruyan's trained human elite warriors + the cutting-edge technology left by the doctor. You stand between the castle and the outpost, possessing a third force completely different from both.

**The old forces will eventually perish, and a new legend is being written.** But the ending only gives you a blank line: "The castle and the outpost are both waiting for your decision."—this decision, the game leaves to every player's imagination.

---

### 4.4 Space Endings

#### Ending 6: Flying to Space
Board the rocket alone.

The moment the rocket breaks through the atmosphere, you watch the gray earth below gradually shrink into a dim planet. Outside the cabin is endless darkness and stars, you float in weightless space, like a grain of sand thrown into the sea.

This is one of the loneliest endings in the entire game. You escaped the apocalypse, but also lost everything that belongs to "human"—companions, memories, bonds.

**"Maybe one day, you'll find a new home. Maybe not. But at least—you chose to fly."** Escape is brave, but it's also a kind of giving up.

#### Ending 7: The Spark of Hope
Board the rocket with Miss V, Su Xiaohan, and Lilith. Requires all three's favorability to reach 150 (soul mate)—this means at least dozens of conversations, hundreds of gifts, countless times of fighting side by side.

"Miss V finally let go of the handrail she was clutching tightly and said softly: 'Let's go.' Su Xiaohan leaned on your shoulder, tears silently falling. Lilith wiped her eyes and suddenly smiled: 'Hey, we're really going to the sky!'"

**This is the warmest ending in the game.** It's not escape, but—taking everyone you love to find a new home together.

"Outside the cabin is endless darkness and stars. But inside the cabin, there's temperature, there's breathing, there's each other."

#### Ending 8: Stay Here
Don't board the rocket, let Dr. Chen go alone.

The doctor's back is small and lonely in the shadow of the rocket. He turns around and says: "Thank you. For me... live well." Then the rocket takes off and turns into a reverse shooting star.

You stayed. The land under your feet is full of scars, but there's still light in your eyes. After the doctor left, he left the manufacturing equipment for the improved antibody injection—you can get one every day. This means even without the doctor, the world still has a chance to be healed.

**"As long as there's a breath left, the story won't end."—this is the game's highest praise for "staying."**

---

### 4.5 Emotional Endings

#### Ending 9: I'm Not the God of Agriculture
Triggered after completing all three of Mu Miaomiao's quests and going home.

She squats down, cups a handful of soil with both hands, and lets it slowly flow through her fingers. "Later I learned. Every seed, every watering, every seedling... is them watching me from the sky."

This ending doesn't have any heroic battles or grand narratives. It only has the wind blowing wheat waves in the experimental field, the gentle swaying in front of the wreath tombstone, and the blurry hand-drawn family in the diary.

**"The greatest miracle is not weapons, not power, but the sound of a seed breaking through the soil."** This is the quietest ending in the game, and also the closest to "healing."

#### Ending 10: Love and Hate
Triggered after completing all four of the queen's quests and going home.

The queen stands on the castle balcony holding the sunflower pendant. You think of the voice in the voice recorder, think of the leader's silence, think of those people crushed between power and emotion.

"In this apocalypse, a king only loves his queen, is this a kind of romance?"—she asks a question without a standard answer.

The last line of the ending says: "The most luxurious thing in the apocalypse is not power, not supplies, but there are still people worth your hate, and still people worth your love." —**Love and hate are not luxuries, the luxury is "still there."**

#### Ending 12: Elopement
Triggered after completing all of Liu Ruyan's quests.

The off-road vehicle's engine roars, wind blows in through the broken window. Liu Ruyan leans her head against the car window and says softly: "Before the apocalypse, my biggest wish was a graduation trip." You step on the gas: "Then now is our graduation trip." She smiles with her eyes curved.

There are only a few bottles of water, some compressed biscuits, and a crowbar in the trunk. You don't know what's ahead—maybe another dead city, maybe a settlement that preserves the spark of civilization. But you're together.

**"In this collapsing world, the most luxurious thing is not supplies, not weapons, but there's still someone willing to escape with you."**

This is the most "juvenile" ending in the game. It rejects grand narratives, rejects heroism, only grabs one smallest thing—two people, one car, a journey without an end.

---

### 4.6 Legend Endings

#### Ending 13: Mermaid Legend
Triggered after completing all of Old Wolf and the injured mermaid's quests and defeating the giant sea corpse.

The giant sea corpse (600 HP, only remote) sinks into the deep sea. Moonlight shines on the mermaid's newly grown scales, refracting shimmering silver light.

Old Wolf says hoarsely: "After so many years... I've been guarding that warehouse, guarding this secret. Now, I can finally let go."

Before the mermaid dives into the deep sea, she turns around and leaves a lightning scale and a thunder scale. She says: "No matter where you go, the sea will protect you."

**This is a story of a myth reviving in the apocalyptic ruins.** There's a mermaid singing under the deep sea, and on this cursed land, someone understands her song.

---

### 4.7 Time Endings

#### Ending 2: The Unknown Future
Triggered after surviving to day 999.

You find a dusty box in the corner of the base, inside is a backpack glowing with strange light—a dimensional storage backpack (68 slots). This is not an item that can be made by any known technology.

The ending text gives an extremely restrained hint: "It doesn't seem to belong to this world, but it can hold items far beyond common sense." —**999 days, you crossed the limit of apocalyptic survival and touched some kind of power that doesn't belong to this world.**

"In the distance, the voices of other survivors faintly came. The spark of civilization has never been extinguished." —but about the origin of that backpack, the source of that voice, the game doesn't say another word.

## 5. Theme and Depth Analysis

### 5.1 The Order Paradox in the Apocalypse

The core philosophical proposition of *Zombie World* is: **After civilization falls into disorder, what kind of order can let humans survive?**

The game provides two paths—the castle's strongman politics and the outpost's democratic mutual aid—but doesn't give a standard answer. The castle has the queen's true feelings and a moment of respite in the garden; the outpost has assassination quests (the king's fifth quest) and a low-dignity begging system. Both modes have a bright side and dark cracks.

Even crueler: **Both modes rely on a foundation of violence.** The outpost needs Miss V's special forces training, the castle needs the king's dictatorial armed forces. Peace is not the starting point of the apocalypse—force is.

### 5.2 The Gray Area Between Good and Evil

There is no purely good person in the game, and no purely bad person (except Ma San):
- The king: A dictatorial tyrant, but made a sunflower pendant for the queen and erected a monument to fallen soldiers in the garden.
- The leader: A just leader, but opposed his sister's romantic relationship and indirectly forced her to leave and become the queen.
- Dr. Chen: A savior scientist, but may also be the source of the virus spread.
- Old Ma: Self-proclaimed to enforce justice on behalf of heaven, but just continues killing with another excuse.
- Old Zhao: Sometimes gives you ammunition, sometimes fires a stray shot—a man destroyed by war.

**Everyone is both a victim and a perpetrator at the same time.** The king is a slave to power desire, the leader is a prisoner of guilt, the queen is a sacrifice to love, Yumo is a discarded tool person. No one can keep their hands clean in this world.

### 5.3 The Cycle of Power

When the king gives you the crown prince identity card, you've already killed his enemies and handed over his materials. You took power from his hands—but ending three asks a spine-chilling question: "Are you ready to become the next him?"

This is not rhetoric. This is a prophecy.

The power structure itself will devour everyone who enters it. The king was once just a "successful businessman." The leader was once just a "brother who cared about his sister." But when power comes, humanity starts to twist.

**The game is saying: No one is born a tyrant. Tyrants are made step by step by power.**

### 5.4 The Apocalyptic Form of Love

There are five completely different love narratives in the game:
1. **King-Queen**: Possessive love. Locking a person with a crown for three years.
2. **Leader-Sister**: Owing love. Expressing protection with opposition, creating an irreparable rift.
3. **Miss V-Player (Soul Mate)**: Guardian love. Expressing "I don't want to lose you" with training and weapons.
4. **Liu Ruyan-Player**: Escape love. "Before the apocalypse, my biggest wish was a graduation trip"—when the apocalypse strips away all meaning, love returns to the simplest form: there's someone willing to escape with you.
5. **Lilith-Player**: Confession love. A girl who hides all her fear with mischief, finally summoning the courage to say "I like you."

These five loves exist in parallel in the same apocalypse. They have no right or wrong—but in the apocalypse, every one has thorns.

### 5.5 Memory and Forgetting

This is the most obscure but also the most贯穿 theme in the game.

- Mu Miaomiao uses seeds to fight forgetting—"Look, Mom and Dad, I've already grown it."
- The leader uses three years of unsent letters to fight forgetting.
- The queen uses the sunflower pendant to fight forgetting—that's what he was like before becoming king.
- Old Ma uses a ledger to fight forgetting—every name, every account.
- Luluwei uses the name on her chest badge to fight forgetting—"Luluwei," she was once a human.

**In the logic of the apocalypse, forgetting equals a second death.** When no one remembers who you are, what you did, who you loved—your existence completely disappears from history.

The game finally gives the answer in ending nine: Mu Miaomiao's experimental field is not a farm, it's a monument. Every crop is saying the same thing—"We're still here. We still remember."

## 6. Unresolved Foreshadows (Waiting for Sequel)

Developer's note: "The unresolved foreshadows will have to wait for my sequel."—below is a整理 and speculation of the明显 unresolved foreshadows in the game.

### 6.1 The Mystery of the Virus Origin

Old Ma says Dr. Chen "is the source of the virus spread." No other NPC can confirm or deny this. Dr. Chen himself never正面回答 this question. When he boards the rocket and leaves, he takes the secret with him.

**Speculation**: The virus is either a product of Dr. Chen's research institute (a leak incident), or a byproduct of humans trying to create "super soldiers" in biochemical experiments. Zombie gel is collected by multiple forces for research, which hints that there may be deeper military or commercial interests behind the virus.

### 6.2 The Power Source of the Contraband

The apocalyptic contraband in the hidden compartment of the warehouse supermarket—emitting a faint green light, sanity -50 after use, body becomes stronger—its origin and essence have never been explained. After using it, you see a blood moon, an upside-down city, screaming faces.

**Speculation**: The contraband may be a substance from "another dimension" or "non-human civilization." Shadow is responsible for transportation, Ma San is responsible for "handling," the king is responsible for covering up—where is the source of this supply chain? Who is making the contraband? Why does it have this effect?

### 6.3 Under the Deep Sea

The existence of the mermaid and the giant sea corpse proves: the energy of the apocalypse not only created zombies, but also awakened sleeping ancient creatures. But why are there mermaids in the deep sea? Why does Old Wolf know the secret of the scales? The mermaid says "I'm going back to the deep sea, that's my home"—are there other mermaids in the deep sea? Are there bigger threats?

### 6.4 Old Wolf's Identity

Old Wolf is a lonely elderly person in the residential area, but he knows the existence of the mermaid, knows the secret of the scales, knows how to feed scales to heal the mermaid's wound. His shotgun is not an ordinary shotgun—it played a key role in fighting the giant sea corpse.

**Speculation**: Old Wolf may be a person related to deep-sea research before the apocalypse—marine biologist, retired navy personnel, or a member of a secret project. He chose to live alone in the residential area to guard the mermaid in the warehouse.

### 6.5 The Dimensional Storage Backpack

In ending two, the 68-slot dimensional storage backpack that appears after surviving 999 days "doesn't seem to belong to this world." Where did it come from? Why did it appear after 999 days? Who left it?

**Speculation**: This may hint that there is some kind of "external force" or "higher civilization" observing the progress of the apocalypse in the game. 999 days is a threshold—you proved that you are qualified to receive help that doesn't belong to this world.

### 6.6 Old Ma's Dark History

Old Ma proclaims himself "enforcing justice on behalf of heaven," but what is his own role? He calls himself "the accountant on the ledger," but he has all the information about the old world's dark forces—didn't he participate?

**Speculation**: Old Ma may have been a member of the dark forces from the beginning (accountant), but for some reason (unfair division of spoils? Abandoned?)转而清算 all his accomplices. His revenge is not justice, but another form of power struggle.

### 6.7 Forces Beyond the Castle and Outpost

The wasteland in the game obviously has more than three settlements: the castle, the outpost, and the island. The robbers, black market merchants, and wandering survivors encountered in random events—where do they come from?

**Speculation**: The sequel may expand to a wider world map—other cities, other forces, other "kings" and "leaders." Doom Castle and Dawn Outpost are just the tip of the iceberg on this wasteland.

### 6.8 More of the Giant Sea Corpse's Kind

The mermaid says the giant sea corpse was "awakened by the energy of the apocalypse." If one can be awakened, then what about other giant creatures in the depths of the sea? The ocean covers 71% of the Earth's surface—there may be more terrifying existences sleeping below than all the threats on land combined.

### 6.9 The Rugby Zombie and the Secrets of University Town

The "rugby zombie" fought in Liu Ruyan's quest is the infected school rugby team captain. What else is underground in University Town? What's hidden in the principal's office in the administration building? Why did the principal leave an off-road vehicle and keys?

### 6.10 After 999 Days

Ending two only says "in the distance, the voices of other survivors faintly came," no more information. What is the world like after 999 days? Have zombies started to mutate? Have new forces risen? Does that dimensional storage backpack mean some kind of "breakthrough" possibility?

## Conclusion

*Zombie World* is not a game about zombies, it's a game about **people**.

Zombies are just the backdrop. The real story happens in those dialogues in the ruins, in that trust exchanged with cigarettes, in that感情 torn apart and sewn back together by an unsent letter.

It raises questions but doesn't give answers—can power not corrupt? Can love not possess? Can order not be violent? Can justice not become another excuse for killing?

**Every question is a seed buried in the apocalyptic wasteland. And the answer—may break through the soil in the sequel.**
